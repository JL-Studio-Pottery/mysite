Meet Julie LoRusso

Julie LoRusso works from her garden home studio in Virginia. Except for an introduction to the wheel pottery class through the community center, she is a self-taught ceramic artist. She enjoys combining various methods of working with clay, adorning wheel-thrown forms with sculpted details as well as using slip casting and hand-building techniques. While living in London, she was inspired by the stacking tulipieres she saw on a trip to Amsterdam. She also discovered sprig molds and made her first one of a lion head from a Poundland toy.

Works

Vases

Julie creates one of a kind vases with a focus on tulipieres created to highlight every stem and to be a work of art when not displaying fresh flowers.

Bough Pots

Combing the form of a cache pot with the function of a tulipiere or flower brick creating unique and beautiful works.

Cache Pots

A unique interpretation on cache pots with classic decorative details including handles, lion heads, intaglios, and floral elements
